# CLARK Use Of Funds And Milestones

## Purpose

This document provides a more investor-ready summary of how CLARK expects to use capital and what milestones that capital is intended to unlock.

It is a companion to:

- `docs/financials/startup-costs-first-pass.md`
- `docs/financials/use-of-funds-12-to-18-months.md`

## Capital Use Philosophy

CLARK should use capital to prove a sequence, not to fund a vague industrial vision.

The first capital raised should primarily prove:

1. the training-first launch
2. the Clark Courses education line
3. the first Clarkware development and testing loop
4. the first credible partner-facility development pathway
5. the Hamilton co-location model at `24 Clark Ave`

## Hamilton Co-Location Plan At 24 Clark Ave

The Hamilton operating model is no longer abstract. CLARK and Niagara Assembly are expected to co-locate within the `24 Clark Ave` property.

### Current Working Space Plan

- total first-phase co-location footprint under active planning: about `4,500 sq ft`
- CLARK leads the charge in renovating and activating the front two portions of that space
- the back third of the `4,500 sq ft` footprint is reserved for Niagara Assembly's specialized low-batch testing and flashing hub

### Functional Intent Of The First-Phase Space

The front two portions led by CLARK should support:

- training delivery
- Clark Courses cohorts
- classroom and demonstration activity
- sample-library and teaching-material storage
- Clarkware pilot-support and visible digital workflow surfaces
- early mixed-use teaching and testing activity that strengthens the Assembly Centre story

The back-third Niagara Assembly area should support:

- specialized low-batch testing
- firmware flashing and related technical workflows
- higher-trust technical support capability that complements both training and Clarkware

### Additional Main-Level Opportunity

The building also reportedly contains about `5,000 additional sq ft` on the main level that Niagara Assembly may independently attempt to secure and rent as well.

At this stage, CLARK should treat that additional area as upside and option value, not as a guaranteed part of the launch footprint.

## Use Of Funds Categories

### 1. Founders, Early Staff, And Contractors

Capital use:

- founder compensation
- early staff hiring
- contractor and specialist support

Why it matters:

- CLARK is not a one-person project
- underfunding the human layer is the fastest path to a stalled launch

### 2. Training Services And Clark Courses

Capital use:

- instructor path and train-the-trainer support
- curriculum packaging
- course materials and sample-library development
- launch logistics for public and private cohorts

Why it matters:

- this is CLARK's first direct revenue line
- this is also the first visible proof that the company can deliver real value

### 3. Clarkware Development And Testing

Capital use:

- Niagara pilot build
- product and engineering work
- infrastructure, integration, and reporting
- demo and deployment support

Why it matters:

- Clarkware is a key long-term differentiator
- but it must be proven through real work, not promises

### 4. Hamilton Co-Location Activation At 24 Clark Ave

Capital use:

- CLARK-led renovation and activation of the front two portions of the `4,500 sq ft` launch footprint
- teaching benches, classroom infrastructure, and demonstration space
- selected testing-capable infrastructure where it directly supports CLARK's launch model
- shared IT, AV, safety, and space-readiness work tied to CLARK's active use

Why it matters:

- the physical environment is part of the credibility story
- CLARK is not only launching courses, but an operating centre with visible industrial seriousness

### 5. Partner-Facility Development

Capital use:

- facility review and diligence
- node onboarding design
- documentation, standards, and relationship development

Why it matters:

- CLARK's model depends on a network, not only one site

### 6. Company Infrastructure, Legal, Insurance, And Overhead

Capital use:

- legal and accounting setup
- insurance
- operational tooling
- business development support
- investor and partner materials

## Milestone Structure

### Milestone 1: Hamilton Site Control And Co-Location Definition

Target outcome:

- CLARK's launch footprint inside `24 Clark Ave` is defined clearly
- front two portions of the `4,500 sq ft` area are assigned to CLARK-led activation
- Niagara Assembly's reserved back-third testing and flashing hub is defined clearly enough to plan around
- rights, responsibilities, and cost-sharing principles are documented

### Milestone 2: Space Activation And Training Readiness

Target outcome:

- teaching-capable and demonstration-capable portions of the space are usable
- first benches, storage, sample-library, and classroom infrastructure are in place
- instructor path and training materials are ready for launch

### Milestone 3: Training Revenue Launch

Target outcome:

- first IPC and Clark Courses cohorts delivered
- repeatable scheduling, registration, and cohort operations established
- first revenue generated

### Milestone 4: Clarkware Pilot Proof

Target outcome:

- Niagara pilot is live
- first evidence-capture path works
- reporting and messaging loop is demonstrated
- support burden and next-step pricing logic are clearer

### Milestone 5: Integrated Teaching And Testing Centre Credibility

Target outcome:

- CLARK-led teaching and demonstration area is operating
- Niagara Assembly's back-third testing and flashing hub is functioning as a complementary technical anchor
- the combined site can be presented credibly as an early teaching-capable and testing-capable assembly centre

### Milestone 6: Partner-Facility Development Proof

Target outcome:

- at least one credible partner-facility pathway is under active development
- onboarding economics and support burden are clearer

## Raise Narrative

The cleanest raise story is not:

"we need money for a lot of things."

It is:

"we are financing the launch of a training-first industrial platform, anchored in a defined co-location footprint at 24 Clark Ave, with capital tied to training revenue, Clark Courses launch, Clarkware pilot proof, and the first repeatable partner-facility pathway."

## Current Recommendation

If CLARK wants a serious raise story that matches the current plan, the most coherent framing is:

- capital to activate the CLARK-led portions of `24 Clark Ave`
- capital to launch training and Clark Courses
- capital to fund Clarkware through proof, not theater
- capital to build the first organizational layer around the network model

The more CLARK chooses to lead real space activation and carry early staffing seriously, the more reasonable a larger raise becomes.
