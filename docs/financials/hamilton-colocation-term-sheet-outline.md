# Hamilton Co-Location Term Sheet Outline

## Document Purpose

This document is a negotiation-ready outline for the proposed co-location arrangement between CLARK and Niagara Assembly at `24 Clark Ave`, Hamilton, Ontario.

It is designed to be:

- specific enough to support real term negotiation
- flexible enough to refine as legal, operating, and capital details mature
- strongly structured so terms can be updated without rewriting the entire document

## Document Status

- Status: `Working term sheet outline`
- Intended use: `Founder negotiation, partner alignment, legal drafting input`
- Legal effect: `Non-binding unless and until definitive agreements say otherwise`

### 1.2 Niagara Assembly

### 2.1 Primary Address

## 3. Space Allocation

### 3.1 CLARK-Led Portions

Current working position:

- CLARK leads renovation and activation of the front two portions of the first-phase `4,500 sq ft` footprint

Intended use includes:

- IPC training delivery
- Clark Courses public and private cohorts
- teaching and demonstration space
- sample-library and curriculum storage
- mixed teaching-and-testing use where aligned with CLARK's launch model
- Clarkware-visible workflow surfaces and related demonstration capability

### 3.2 Niagara Assembly Reserved Portion

Current working position:

- the back third of the first-phase `4,500 sq ft` footprint is reserved for Niagara Assembly's specialized low-batch testing and flashing hub

Intended use includes:

- low-batch testing
- firmware flashing
- technical validation support
- specialized activities that complement the front-of-house training and demonstration footprint

### 3.3 Shared Areas

To be defined:

- entrances and access paths
- washrooms and break areas

- shared utilities and network areas
- common safety and circulation space

## 4. Core Commercial Intent

### 4.1 Co-Location Objective

The co-location arrangement is intended to create an early teaching-capable and testing-capable assembly centre that supports:

- CLARK's training-first launch
- Clark Courses delivery
- visible industrial credibility for CLARK and Niagara Assembly
- future Clarkware demonstration and pilot support
- Niagara Assembly's specialized low-batch technical workflows

### 4.2 Business Rationale

The parties intend the arrangement to:

- accelerate CLARK's launch
- strengthen Niagara Assembly's workforce and technical support position
- create a regionally credible operating site
- establish a replicable beta model for future CLARK-linked facilities

## 7. Cost Sharing

### 7.1 Base Commercial Model

Current working position:

- Niagara Assembly hosts the footprint
- Niagara Assembly charges CLARK nominal rent and equipment-use fees
- CLARK may contribute meaningful launch capital toward fit-out and activation of CLARK-led portions of the site

### 7.2 Fit-Out Cost Sharing

To be negotiated under one of these general structures:

#### Option A: CLARK-Light Contribution

- CLARK funds only training-relevant and pilot-relevant fit-out
- Niagara Assembly funds the rest of the facility and technical hub scope

#### Option B: Shared Strategic Contribution

- CLARK and Niagara Assembly both contribute to a stronger mixed teaching-and-testing launch footprint
- cost sharing is tied to who benefits from each category of spend

#### Option C: CLARK-Heavy Contribution

- CLARK contributes materially beyond training-only scope
- stronger rights, protections, and economic consideration should apply in return

### 7.3 Rent And Occupancy Charges

Current working position:

- nominal rent and equipment-use fees charged by Niagara Assembly to CLARK

To be specified:

- fixed monthly base amount
- utility treatment
- common-area cost treatment
- escalation or step-up schedule

## 8. Revenue Sharing

### 8.1 Training Revenue Share

Current working position:

- 1 to 2 delivered courses per month: CAD 1,250 fixed monthly facility fee plus 12% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs
- 3 to 4 delivered courses per month: CAD 2,500 fixed monthly facility fee plus 10% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs
- 5 or more delivered courses per month: CAD 4,000 fixed monthly facility fee plus 8% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs

### 8.2 Contribution Margin Definition

Current working position:

- direct instructional costs include instructor compensation, instructor travel and lodging where applicable, course-specific consumables and training materials, and third-party certification or course-delivery fees tied directly to the class
- broader CLARK overhead is excluded

### 8.3 Non-Training Revenue Treatment

To be negotiated:

- Clark Courses public and private cohort treatment
- partner-venue training revenue treatment
- licensed delivery revenue treatment
- Clarkware demonstration or pilot-related revenue treatment if any
