# CLARK Startup Costs First Pass

## Purpose

This document provides a first-pass review of the startup capital CLARK is likely to need to get off the ground.

It is intentionally broader and more conservative than the narrower product financial plans. The goal is to help answer a practical founder question:

How much money does CLARK need to raise or borrow to launch credibly without starving the company in its first year?

## Scope

This memo covers startup and launch costs for CLARK itself, including:

- company setup and operating infrastructure
- training-services launch
- Clark Courses launch support
- Clarkware pilot build and testing
- early partner-facility development work
- go-to-market, diligence, and working capital

This memo does **not** assume CLARK is solely funding Niagara Assembly's Hamilton cleanup and full facility outfitting.

However, it does include a separate scenario in which CLARK contributes meaningful capital toward outfitting the Clark Ave space as a teaching-capable and testing-capable assembly centre.

## Currency And Planning Standard

- Primary planning currency: `CAD`
- Standard used here: `first-pass launch planning`, not audited budgeting
- Philosophy: prefer realistic reserves and contingency to founder optimism

## Important Framing

CLARK is not launching one thing. It is launching several interdependent pieces at once:

- a training-first revenue engine
- a Clark Courses education line
- a Clarkware pilot and development path
- an operating layer for partner-facility development

Because of that, undercapitalization is a bigger risk than carrying a prudent reserve.

There is another major framing point:

The earlier version of this memo was still too project-centric. If CLARK is truly going to support 2 to 3 founders, add a couple of early staff, and rely on contractors where internal capability is thin, then staffing becomes the dominant startup-cost category rather than a side note.

## Staffing And Contractor Reality Check

If CLARK is staffed seriously, the capital need changes materially.

### Founder Compensation

For planning purposes, founders should be treated as real operating cost, even if some compensation is deferred or partly sacrificed.

Working loaded annual planning band per founder:

- `CAD 100,000 to CAD 175,000`

This reflects:

- modest salary or draw
- payroll burden or equivalent
- benefits allowance or substitute buffer
- travel and business-use friction that founders often absorb informally

Working 12-month founder totals:

- `2 founders`: `CAD 200,000 to CAD 350,000`
- `3 founders`: `CAD 300,000 to CAD 525,000`

### Early Staff

The business plan already implies the need for roles such as:

- network operations lead
- customer development lead
- training and quality lead
- software tools lead
- cross-border logistics coordinator

CLARK may not hire all of these immediately, but even 2 to 3 real early hires create a meaningful cost base.

Working loaded annual planning band per early staff member:

- `CAD 95,000 to CAD 160,000`

Working 12-month staff totals:

- `2 early staff`: `CAD 190,000 to CAD 320,000`
- `3 early staff`: `CAD 285,000 to CAD 480,000`
- `4 early staff`: `CAD 380,000 to CAD 640,000`

### Contractors And Specialists

Contractors should not be treated as a small add-on. In the current CLARK model they are likely to cover:

- instructors
- curriculum packaging and design support
- software and integration work
- legal drafting
- finance and bookkeeping support
- selective technical specialists

Working 12-month contractor band:

- `CAD 100,000 to CAD 350,000`

### Staffing Implication

If CLARK tries to operate for 12 months with:

- `2 founders`
- `2 early staff`
- meaningful contractor support

the staffing-and-contractor layer alone can reasonably land in the range of:

- `CAD 490,000 to CAD 1,020,000`

If CLARK instead carries:

- `3 founders`
- `3 to 4 early staff`
- serious contractor support

the staffing-and-contractor layer can reasonably land in the range of:

- `CAD 685,000 to CAD 1,515,000`

That is before meaningful contingency and before any material Clark Ave contribution.

## Startup Cost Categories Beyond Staffing

### 1. Company Formation And Core Operating Setup

This category includes:

- legal setup and structuring
- founder and advisor documentation
- contract templates for training, partner facilities, and pilots
- accounting, bookkeeping, and tax setup
- CRM, document, and operations software
- basic branding, web, and communications infrastructure

Working range:

- `CAD 15,000 to CAD 40,000`

## 2. Insurance, Compliance, And Risk Setup

This category includes:

- general liability
- training and instructor liability coverage
- E&O / professional coverage where needed
- cyber and data-risk coverage for Clarkware and records
- policy reviews and broker fees

Working range:

- `CAD 12,000 to CAD 35,000`

This category is easy to underestimate and should not be squeezed unrealistically.

## 3. Training Launch Setup

This category includes:

- instructor recruiting and deposits
- EPTAC / train-the-trainer path costs
- sample boards, components, and training materials
- printed materials, guides, and delivery kits
- consumables for hands-on delivery
- scheduling, registration, and payment workflow setup
- initial travel and lodging for instructors where needed

Working range:

- `CAD 30,000 to CAD 85,000`

This category matters because training is CLARK's first direct revenue line and cannot be launched in a sloppy or underprepared state.

## 4. Clark Courses Launch And Curriculum Packaging

This category includes:

- curriculum cleanup and standardization
- deck and teaching-material polish
- instructor briefing materials
- cohort packaging and offer design
- sample-library expansion
- early marketing collateral for public and private cohorts

Working range:

- `CAD 15,000 to CAD 45,000`

This is separate from core training delivery because Clark Courses is now a standalone education line, not just a side layer around IPC.

## 5. Clarkware Pilot Build And Early Software Development

This category includes:

- engineering and product work
- pilot deployment support
- UI and reporting iteration
- messaging and identity setup
- integration work for the first diagnostics instrument path
- local infrastructure and storage setup
- demo and pilot support labor

Working range:

- `CAD 50,000 to CAD 175,000`

This range is deliberately wide because the real number depends heavily on whether most of the work is founder-built, contractor-built, or a mix.

## 6. Pilot Hardware, IT, And Technical Environment

This category includes:

- workstations or workstation upgrades
- local server or node hardware if needed
- networking and backup hardware
- pilot-support displays, peripherals, cameras, or capture devices
- bench-integration accessories
- software subscriptions and hosting for the pilot path

Working range:

- `CAD 10,000 to CAD 40,000`

This does not assume CLARK is buying all Niagara production equipment. It assumes CLARK pays for the pilot-specific enabling environment it needs.

## 7. Partner-Facility Development And Diligence

This category includes:

- travel for facility review and relationship building
- diligence and qualification work
- onboarding design and documentation
- partnership development time
- early commercial and quality-system support

Working range:

- `CAD 15,000 to CAD 50,000`

Even if the first node relationships are close to home, partner development still consumes real time and cash.

## 8. Go-To-Market And Business Development

This category includes:

- outreach and sales support
- partner-development materials
- investor and lender materials
- local market-development activity
- light event, meeting, and travel costs

Working range:

- `CAD 15,000 to CAD 50,000`

## 9. Founding Operating Buffer And Working Capital

This category includes:

- founder living support or draw buffer where needed
- operating float before customer cash arrives
- timing gaps between upfront spend and first revenue
- emergency flexibility when assumptions fail

Working range:

- `CAD 50,000 to CAD 175,000`

This is the category founders most often under-budget. It is also the category most likely to determine whether CLARK survives long enough to prove its first model.

## 10. Contingency Reserve

Recommended minimum contingency:

- `10% to 20%` of launch spend before contingency

Working range:

- `CAD 25,000 to CAD 100,000`

## CLARK-Only Launch Scenarios

These scenarios exclude full Niagara Assembly cleanup and full Clark Ave outfitting.

### Scenario A: Bare-Minimum Project Launch

This assumes:

- founder-heavy execution
- minimal paid software help
- tight training launch
- very limited working-capital cushion

Estimated range:

- `CAD 212,000 to CAD 315,000`

Interpretation:

- possible, but fragile
- vulnerable to any delay in instructor readiness, pilot development, or first-course fill rates
- not suitable if CLARK intends to carry 2 to 3 founders and a couple of early staff in a serious way

### Scenario B: Lean Company Launch

This assumes:

- `2 founders`
- `1 to 2 early hires`
- meaningful contractor use
- credible training launch
- serious Clarkware pilot work
- real insurance, legal, and working-capital support

Estimated range:

- `CAD 700,000 to CAD 1,250,000`

Interpretation:

- this is the first range that starts to look like an actual company launch rather than a tightly improvised project launch

### Scenario C: Recommended Working Company Launch

This assumes:

- `2 to 3 founders`
- `2 to 3 early staff`
- meaningful contractor support
- credible training launch
- serious Clarkware pilot work
- partner-development activity
- real insurance and legal setup
- a working cash cushion rather than a survival-level float

Estimated range:

- `CAD 1,200,000 to CAD 2,100,000`

Interpretation:

- this is the most realistic first-pass raise target if CLARK wants room to build a team, absorb delays, and prove more than one layer of the model

### Scenario D: Strongly Buffered Platform Launch

This assumes:

- `3 founders`
- `3 to 4 early staff`
- more complete software and pilot support
- stronger curriculum packaging
- heavier contractor and specialist use
- more working capital
- room for delays and iteration

Estimated range:

- `CAD 2,100,000 to CAD 3,500,000`

Interpretation:

- expensive, but far more resilient
- best suited to a raise intended to build real organizational capacity and avoid an early return to market

## Shared Clark Ave Outfitting Scenario

This is the separate scenario where CLARK helps Niagara Assembly outfit the Clark Ave space as a teaching-capable and testing-capable assembly centre.

This is **in addition** to CLARK's own operating startup costs.

### Potential Shared-Outfitting Cost Categories

- training-room preparation
- teaching benches and work surfaces
- electrical and lighting improvements
- storage, shelving, and material handling
- safety equipment and ESD provisions
- testing benches and light diagnostics instrumentation
- classroom AV and presentation equipment
- networking and local IT improvements
- sample-library and demonstration hardware
- minor facility improvements needed for mixed training and testing use

### CLARK Contribution Cases

#### Light Contribution

CLARK contributes only to directly training-relevant and pilot-relevant elements.

Estimated CLARK contribution:

- `CAD 50,000 to CAD 125,000`

#### Shared Strategic Contribution

CLARK helps fund a stronger mixed teaching and testing environment that also supports early Clarkware and future assembly-centre credibility.

Estimated CLARK contribution:

- `CAD 125,000 to CAD 300,000`

#### Heavy Contribution

CLARK behaves almost like a co-sponsor of the space buildout.

Estimated CLARK contribution:

- `CAD 300,000 to CAD 600,000+`

This should be approached cautiously unless CLARK receives very clear long-term rights, operating control, or economic benefit in return.

## Combined Raise / Borrow Scenarios

### If CLARK does **not** materially fund Clark Ave outfitting

Recommended target:

- `CAD 1,000,000 to CAD 2,250,000`

Reasoning:

- enough to support founder and early-staff cost in a serious way
- enough to launch training credibly
- enough to support Clarkware pilot development
- enough to begin partner-facility development
- enough working capital to avoid immediate strain

### If CLARK makes a **light** Clark Ave contribution

Recommended target:

- `CAD 1,100,000 to CAD 2,400,000`

### If CLARK makes a **shared strategic** Clark Ave contribution

Recommended target:

- `CAD 1,300,000 to CAD 2,750,000`

### If CLARK becomes a **heavy co-funder** of Clark Ave outfitting

Recommended target:

- `CAD 1,800,000 to CAD 4,000,000+`

At that point the financing story changes materially. CLARK is no longer just funding its own launch; it is helping capitalize physical infrastructure.

### Where A `CAD 5M` Raise Starts To Make Sense

An early raise around `CAD 5M` is not the number I would call necessary for a basic launch.

It starts to make sense if CLARK wants to do several of the following at once:

- carry `3 founders` with credible compensation
- add `3 to 5 early staff` sooner rather than later
- fund meaningful contractor and engineering support
- contribute heavily to Clark Ave outfitting
- carry `18 to 24 months` of runway rather than a tight 12-month build
- avoid coming back to market before the training, Clarkware, and node-development layers are all more mature

In other words:

- `CAD 5M` is a bold platform-build number
- it is not the minimum viable launch number

## Recommendation

Based on the current documentation and a more honest staffing view, my independent conclusion is:

- `CAD 1.2M to CAD 2.2M` is the most credible first-pass range for a properly staffed CLARK launch over roughly 12 to 18 months

If CLARK intends to contribute meaningfully to Clark Ave outfitting as a teaching-capable and testing-capable assembly centre, then the more realistic first-pass target becomes:

- `CAD 1.5M to CAD 3.0M`

If CLARK wants to behave more like an aggressively capitalized platform build with deeper staffing, stronger runway, and a heavier physical-infrastructure contribution, then a target in the neighborhood of:

- `CAD 4M to CAD 5M`

becomes understandable.

## Borrowing Versus Raising

### Better candidates for borrowing

- equipment with durable resale value
- fit-out items directly tied to facility use
- limited working-capital bridge against visible training revenue

### Better candidates for equity or flexible founder capital

- curriculum development
- software development and pilot iteration
- early business development
- partner-facility development
- contingency and runway

Reason:

- these items are harder to collateralize and more uncertain in timing

## Decision Questions Before Finalizing A Raise Target

1. How much of Clarkware build cost will be founder-supplied labor versus paid external labor?
2. Will CLARK pay only for training and pilot-specific space enablement, or for a broader Clark Ave outfitting scope?
3. Does CLARK need to support founder living costs from startup capital?
4. How much delay tolerance does CLARK want before training reaches a stable monthly cadence?
5. What rights does CLARK receive if it materially funds Clark Ave improvements?

## Current Bottom Line

If the goal is to get CLARK off the ground while paying 2 to 3 founders, adding a couple of early staff, using contractors honestly, and avoiding a starvation launch, then anything below about `CAD 1M` starts to look too tight.

If the goal is to launch CLARK credibly, support training and Clarkware properly, and preserve some ability to co-fund a meaningful Clark Ave buildout with Niagara Assembly, the safer planning range is:

- `CAD 1.5M to CAD 3.0M`

My independent view is that `CAD 5M` is not required for the minimum launch described in the current docs, but it is a defensible target if the ambition is to:

- build CLARK as a genuinely staffed platform company
- carry stronger runway
- absorb execution delays without panic
- and contribute materially to physical infrastructure rather than only to program launch
