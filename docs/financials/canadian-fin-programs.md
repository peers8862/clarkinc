# canadian-fin-programs

## Purpose

This document compiles Canadian regional, provincial, and federal programs with a meaningful monetary component that could serve as early-stage financing sources for CLARK, either alone or in combination.

This is not a generic grants list. It is a practical finance memo for CLARK's current Ontario and Hamilton-centered launch.

## Research Standard

- Focus: programs with a strong monetary component such as grants, loans, repayable contributions, loan guarantees, or tax credits
- Geography bias: Hamilton, Ontario, southern Ontario, and Canada-wide programs usable by an Ontario-based venture
- Use case: early-stage company formation, training launch, Clarkware development, co-location activation, equipment purchase, and manufacturing capability buildout
- Verification date for this memo: `2026-03-28`

## Executive Read

The strongest current Canadian financing stack for CLARK is not a single program. It is a staged combination:

1. founder-friendly debt or loan-guarantee capital for launch and working capital
2. tax-credit recovery for technical development and Ontario-based R&D
3. project-based manufacturing or commercialization support once CLARK crosses staffing, operating-history, and project-size thresholds

The best immediate-fit programs for CLARK appear to be:

- `SR&ED`
- `Ontario Innovation Tax Credit`
- `BDC Start-up Financing`
- `Canada Small Business Financing Program`
- `Futurpreneur`, if one or more founders qualify on age and structure

The best threshold-dependent programs for later in the first serious operating phase appear to be:

- `FedDev Ontario funding for businesses in southern Ontario`
- `AMIC`
- `Ontario Made Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit`
- `Ontario Business-Research Institute Tax Credit`
- `Regional Defence Investment Initiative`, if CLARK chooses a defence or dual-use commercialization path

The best small-dollar local program currently visible is:

- `Hamilton Starter Company Plus`

## Immediate-Fit Programs

### 1. Federal: SR&ED

- Official source:
  - https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/scientific-research-experimental-development-tax-incentive-program.html
  - https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/scientific-research-experimental-development-tax-incentive-program/sred-claim/investment-tax-credit.html
  - https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/scientific-research-experimental-development-tax-incentive-program/submit-sred-claim.html
- Monetary form: tax deduction plus investment tax credit
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - Clarkware development
  - technical process-development work
  - possible test, workflow, and manufacturing-software experimentation done in Canada
- Core amounts:
  - basic federal SR&ED ITC: `15%`
  - enhanced rate for many qualifying CCPCs: `35%` on up to `CAD 3 million` of qualified expenditures
- Refundability:
  - many qualifying CCPC claims are refundable
  - non-qualifying corporations are generally at the lower, non-refundable rate
- Deadline:
  - for corporations, the SR&ED reporting deadline is `12 months after the T2 filing due date`
  - the T2 filing due date is generally `6 months after tax year end`
  - practical example if CLARK uses a `December 31` year end:
    - T2 due: `June 30` of the following year
    - SR&ED reporting deadline: `June 30` one year after that
- Processing timing from CRA:
  - claims are generally processed within `60 calendar days` if accepted as filed
  - refundable claims selected for review are generally completed within `180 calendar days`
- Contacts:
  - CRA business enquiries: `1-800-959-5525`
  - CRA TTY: `1-800-665-0354`
- CLARK action notes:
  - this is one of the highest-priority programs to design around early
  - CLARK should set up contemporaneous technical documentation and expense tracking from day one if Clarkware or manufacturing-process R&D starts in Canada

### 2. Provincial: Ontario Innovation Tax Credit

- Official source:
  - https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontario-innovation-tax-credit
- Monetary form: refundable Ontario corporate income tax credit
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - stacks conceptually with federal SR&ED for Ontario R&D work
  - can improve cash recovery on Canadian technical work beyond federal support alone
- Core amount:
  - `8%` refundable credit on qualified Ontario SR&ED expenditures
- Eligibility highlights:
  - corporation must have a permanent establishment in Ontario
  - must carry out eligible SR&ED in Ontario
  - must be eligible for the federal SR&ED ITC
  - must file federal form `T661`
- Deadline:
  - claimed with the `T2` return using Ontario `Schedule 566`
- Contact:
  - CRA: `1-800-959-5525`
  - TTY: `1-800-665-0354`
- CLARK action notes:
  - treat this as a paired program with SR&ED, not a standalone grant
  - if CLARK keeps core product-development work in Ontario, this should stay in the default tax-credit stack

### 3. Federal: BDC Start-up Financing

- Official source:
  - https://www.bdc.ca/en/financing/starting-business-loan
  - https://www.bdc.ca/en/contact-us
- Monetary form: direct loan
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - can fund launch costs, fees, marketing, consultants, and some early operating setup
  - useful as solitary launch capital or matching capital alongside grants and tax credits
- Core amount:
  - up to `CAD 150,000`
- Current fit conditions shown by BDC:
  - based in Canada
  - at least `12 months` in business
  - generating revenue
  - good credit history
- Repayment feature worth noting:
  - interest-only payments available for up to the first `12 months`
- Deadline:
  - rolling / no published intake deadline
- Contacts:
  - BDC Client Contact Centre: `1-877-232-2269`
  - head office contact page: https://www.bdc.ca/en/contact-us
- CLARK action notes:
  - not a pure pre-revenue founder instrument
  - becomes more credible after CLARK has some revenue visibility from training or signed customer work
  - likely better as bridge or working-capital support than as the very first dollar

### 4. Federal: Canada Small Business Financing Program

- Official source:
  - https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/canada-small-business-financing-program/en
  - https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/canada-small-business-financing-program/en/find-loan-your-small-business/canada-small-business-financing-program-guidelines
- Monetary form: government-backed loan guarantee delivered through lenders
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - useful for equipment, leaseholds, IP, working capital, and certain property-related expenditures
  - can support physical activation and equipment purchase more directly than many innovation grants
- Core amounts:
  - maximum overall financing: `CAD 1.15 million`
  - up to `CAD 1 million` for term loans
  - up to `CAD 150,000` for lines of credit
- Eligibility snapshot:
  - for-profit, not-for-profit, and charitable small businesses in Canada
  - gross annual revenues of `CAD 10 million` or less
- Delivery model:
  - apply through participating lenders, not directly to government for underwriting
- Deadline:
  - ongoing through lenders
- Contacts:
  - CSBFP Directorate: `csbfp-pfpec@ised-isde.gc.ca`
  - toll-free: `1-866-959-1699`
- CLARK action notes:
  - likely one of the most practical tools for benches, equipment, leasehold improvements, and space activation financing
  - should be part of CLARK's early lender conversations once the Hamilton scope is defined tightly enough

### 5. Federal / National Non-Profit: Futurpreneur Core Startup Program

- Official source:
  - https://futurpreneur.ca/en/offering/core-startup/
  - https://futurpreneur.ca/en/contact/
- Monetary form: loan plus mentorship
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - can be strong founder-level early capital if an eligible founder fits the age criteria
  - especially valuable if CLARK wants non-dilutive founder launch money plus structured mentoring
- Core amount:
  - up to `CAD 75,000`
  - current public description says this includes Futurpreneur financing together with BDC financing
- Eligibility snapshot:
  - founders aged `18 to 39`
  - intended for those ready to start or buy a business in Canada
- Deadline:
  - rolling / no fixed public deadline shown
- Contacts:
  - general email: `info@futurpreneur.ca`
  - toll-free: `1-800-464-2923`
  - Ontario national office: `33 Yonge Street, Suite 1100, Toronto, ON M5E 1G4`
- CLARK action notes:
  - only useful if one or more founders qualify personally
  - if eligible, this should be examined immediately because it can be one of the cleanest first-cheque sources

## Threshold-Dependent Programs

### 6. Federal Regional: FedDev Ontario Funding For Businesses In Southern Ontario

- Official source:
  - https://feddev-ontario.canada.ca/en/funding-southern-ontario
  - https://feddev-ontario.canada.ca/en/funding-southern-ontario/funding-businesses-southern-ontario-what-we-support
  - https://feddev-ontario.canada.ca/en/funding-southern-ontario/funding-businesses-southern-ontario-how-funding-works
- Monetary form: repayable contribution
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - strong potential fit for later-stage Clarkware commercialization, productivity, or manufacturing-system buildout in southern Ontario
  - can be meaningful project-scale capital
- Core amount:
  - normally `CAD 125,000` to `CAD 10 million` per project
- Matching:
  - cost-shared project funding
- Eligibility snapshot:
  - for-profit businesses in southern Ontario
  - `5` to `500` FTEs
  - incorporated and registered in Canada or Ontario for at least the past `3 years`
- Deadline:
  - page says FedDev Ontario is accepting applications
  - no specific fixed deadline published on the general business stream page as of `2026-03-28`
- Contacts:
  - `info@feddevontario.gc.ca`
  - `1-866-593-5505`
- CLARK action notes:
  - attractive, but probably not immediate if CLARK is still below the operating-history or staffing threshold
  - better treated as a post-launch scale-up source than first money

### 7. Provincial: AMIC

- Full name:
  - Advanced Manufacturing and Innovation Competitiveness Stream
- Official source:
  - https://www.ontario.ca/page/regional-development-program-advanced-manufacturing-and-innovation-competitiveness-stream
  - https://www.ontario.ca/document/advanced-manufacturing-and-innovation-competitiveness-stream-program-guidelines
- Monetary form:
  - performance-based loan
  - in limited cases, performance-based grant
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - one of the strongest Ontario manufacturing-project financing tools if CLARK evolves into an eligible advanced-manufacturing applicant
  - relevant to equipment, process upgrades, skills, and manufacturing competitiveness
- Core amounts:
  - up to `15%` of eligible project costs
  - loans up to `CAD 5 million`
  - loans are interest-free during the project period for up to `4 years`
  - up to `30%` of the loan, to a maximum of `CAD 500,000`, may be forgiven if targets are met
  - grants only in specific cases:
    - up to `CAD 500,000` for small rural firms
    - up to `CAD 1.5 million` for strategic FDI or significant reshoring projects
- Key eligibility thresholds:
  - at least `3 years` of operations / financial statements
  - at least `10` FTEs
  - minimum `CAD 500,000` project
  - at least `5` net new and/or upskilled jobs
- Restriction:
  - Ontario says AMIC funding cannot be stacked with other provincial programs
- Current deadlines as of `2026-03-28`:
  - next open intake: `June 30, 2026` to `September 23, 2026`
  - deadline: `September 23, 2026`
  - notification target: `December 17, 2026`
- Contacts:
  - central region for Hamilton / GTHA: `centralregionbas@ontario.ca`
  - business advisory services: `1-800-361-3223`
  - general program email: `rdp@ontario.ca`
- CLARK action notes:
  - likely too large and mature for CLARK's earliest phase
  - should stay on the serious watchlist for a manufacturing-capability project once CLARK crosses the employee and project thresholds

### 8. Provincial: Ontario Made Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit

- Official source:
  - https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontario-made-manufacturing-investment-tax-credit-and-expanded-ontario-made-manufacturing
- Monetary form: refundable tax credit for qualifying CCPCs
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - can support building, machinery, and equipment investment tied to manufacturing or processing in Ontario
  - potentially relevant if CLARK owns or leases and outfits qualifying Ontario manufacturing assets
- Core amounts:
  - current refundable rate for eligible CCPCs: `15%`
  - maximum annual credit: `CAD 3 million`
  - eligible expenditure limit: `CAD 20 million` per taxation year
- Timing rules:
  - enhanced `15%` rate applies where property becomes available for use on or after `May 15, 2025` and before `January 1, 2030`
  - expenditures must be incurred on or before `December 31, 2029`
  - credit is repealed effective `January 1, 2030`
- Contact:
  - CRA: `1-800-959-5525`
  - Ontario Ministry of Finance: `1-866-668-8297`
- CLARK action notes:
  - not first-money financing
  - high-value if CLARK eventually makes qualifying manufacturing capex in Ontario
  - important to track if Clark Ave activation becomes more manufacturing-asset intensive

### 9. Provincial: Ontario Business-Research Institute Tax Credit

- Official source:
  - https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontario-business-research-institute-tax-credit
- Monetary form: refundable tax credit
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - useful if CLARK contracts eligible SR&ED to Ontario universities, colleges, or other eligible research institutes
  - could fit if CLARK works with McMaster or another Ontario institution on manufacturing-software, process, or testing R&D
- Core amounts:
  - `20%` refundable tax credit
  - annual qualified expenditure cap: `CAD 20 million`
  - maximum annual credit: `CAD 4 million`
- Claimed through:
  - Ontario `Schedule 568` and `Schedule 569` with the `T2`
- Contact:
  - CRA: `1-800-959-5525`
  - TTY: `1-800-665-0354`
- CLARK action notes:
  - strong if CLARK chooses institution-linked R&D rather than only internal development
  - can complement, but also complicate, the broader SR&ED planning model

### 10. Federal Regional: Regional Defence Investment Initiative

- Official source:
  - https://feddev-ontario.canada.ca/en/funding-southern-ontario/regional-defence-investment-initiative-southern-ontario
  - https://feddev-ontario.canada.ca/en/funding-southern-ontario/regional-defence-investment-initiative-businesses-who-can-apply
- Monetary form: repayable contribution for businesses
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - only relevant if CLARK intentionally builds a defence or dual-use commercialization path
  - potentially relevant to sensors, training/simulation, cybersecurity, quantum-related, or dual-use manufacturing-support activities
- Core amount:
  - `CAD 125,000` to `CAD 10 million` for businesses
- Eligibility snapshot:
  - southern Ontario
  - incorporated for at least `3 years`
  - minimum `5` FTEs
  - part of or seeking to enter defence supply chains
- Deadline status:
  - if project costs start before `March 31, 2026`, apply by `January 21, 2026` and that window is already closed
  - if project activities start on or after `April 1, 2026`, applications are accepted until funding is fully committed
- Contacts:
  - general FedDev Ontario contact path applies
  - Indigenous applicants email: `indigenous-autochtones@feddevontario.gc.ca`
- CLARK action notes:
  - not a default program for CLARK today
  - should be monitored only if CLARK chooses a real defence-market thesis rather than vague defence-adjacent language

## Local And Small-Dollar Programs

### 11. Hamilton: Starter Company Plus

- Official source:
  - https://investinhamilton.ca/hamilton-business-centre/starter-company-plus/
  - https://investinhamilton.ca/hamilton-business-centre/connect/
- Monetary form: small grant tied to training and mentorship
- Why it matters for CLARK:
  - limited in size, but real local money
  - could be useful if a founder-led Hamilton operating entity qualifies and a founder is carrying the venture full-time
- Core amount:
  - up to `CAD 5,000`
- Current public timeline as of `2026-03-28`:
  - Fall 2026 cohort waitlist open
  - waitlist accepted until `September 7, 2026` at `11:59 PM`
  - applicants can expect coordinator contact in mid-late `August 2026`
  - cohort begins mid-late `September 2026`
- Key restrictions:
  - entrepreneur must be Hamilton resident
  - business must show a Hamilton address
  - minimum `6 months` in operation
  - applicant must work at least `35 hours/week` in the business and not be employed elsewhere
  - applicant must provide a `25%` investment of the grant amount, or `CAD 1,250`
- Contacts:
  - Hamilton Business Centre: `hbc@hamilton.ca`
  - phone: `905-540-6400`
  - address: `71 Main Street West, 1st Floor, Hamilton, Ontario`
- CLARK action notes:
  - too small to matter at venture scale
  - still worth checking if one founder wants to finance a specific local launch activity and qualifies personally

- Monetary form: non-refundable tax credit
- Core amount:

## Stacking Notes

These are the most useful current stacking observations for CLARK:

### High-probability stack

- `BDC` or `CSBFP` for launch debt or equipment financing
- `SR&ED` plus `Ontario Innovation Tax Credit` for Canadian/Ontario R&D recovery
- `Ontario Business-Research Institute Tax Credit` only if CLARK intentionally uses eligible research-institute contracts

### Conditional later-stage stack

- `FedDev Ontario` with federal tax credits and lender capital
- `AMIC` as a stand-alone Ontario project support tool, noting Ontario says AMIC cannot be stacked with other provincial programs
- `OMMITC` for qualifying manufacturing capex

### Caution

- tax credits recover after eligible spend and compliance, not before
- many project programs require CLARK to carry cash, matching funds, or debt capacity first
- the most attractive large-dollar programs generally require more operating history, more employees, and larger project budgets than CLARK may have at the first cheque stage
