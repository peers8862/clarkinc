# CLARK Use Of Funds: 12 To 18 Months

## Purpose

This document translates the startup-cost review into an operating capital plan for CLARK's first 12 to 18 months.

The goal is not just to estimate how much money CLARK needs. The goal is to show how that money gets used over time, what milestones it is intended to unlock, and what raise ranges match different levels of ambition.

## Planning Standard

- Primary currency: `CAD`
- Planning horizon: `12 to 18 months`
- Standard: `founder operating plan`, not audited budget
- Bias: preserve enough runway to survive delays, not just enough to start

## Core Assumptions

This plan assumes:

- CLARK launches training services first
- Clark Courses becomes part of the Year 1 revenue mix
- Clarkware remains primarily a development-and-testing line in the first phase
- partner-facility development begins before broad node expansion
- 2 to 3 founders are carrying real operating load
- 2 to 4 early staff are added over time
- contractors remain important in training, software, legal, finance, and specialist support

## Planning Buckets

The capital plan is easiest to understand in seven buckets:

1. founder compensation
2. early staff compensation
3. contractors and specialists
4. product and program build
5. training and facility-linked launch costs
6. business development and company overhead
7. contingency and runway reserve

## 12-Month Use-Of-Funds View

### 1. Founder Compensation

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 200,000 to CAD 525,000`

This assumes:

- 2 to 3 founders
- modest but real compensation
- no fantasy assumption that founders can indefinitely self-fund the company through unpaid labor

### 2. Early Staff Compensation

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 190,000 to CAD 640,000`

This reflects 2 to 4 early staff across some mix of:

- network operations
- customer development
- training and quality
- software tools
- logistics or coordination

### 3. Contractors And Specialists

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 100,000 to CAD 350,000`

This should include:

- instructors
- curriculum and design support
- software and integration help
- legal drafting
- bookkeeping and finance support
- specialist technical support

### 4. Product And Program Build

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 105,000 to CAD 345,000`

This includes:

- Clarkware pilot build and deployment
- pilot-support hardware and technical environment
- curriculum packaging and Clark Courses launch support
- training-launch setup beyond payroll and contractor fees

### 5. Training And Facility-Linked Launch Costs

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 30,000 to CAD 125,000`

This covers:

- Niagara host-facility economics not already embedded in direct instructional cost
- setup consumables
- teaching materials and sample-library support
- modest launch-specific facility-enablement costs

This does **not** yet assume major Clark Ave co-funding.

### 6. Business Development, Legal, Insurance, And Company Overhead

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 42,000 to CAD 125,000`

This includes:

- company setup
- insurance
- legal and compliance
- software subscriptions
- CRM and operational tooling
- investor, partner, and customer-development support

### 7. Contingency And Runway Reserve

Working 12-month band:

- `CAD 100,000 to CAD 350,000`

This is not optional padding. It is the buffer that keeps CLARK alive when assumptions slip.

## 18-Month Use-Of-Funds View

The 18-month view matters because many companies that raise for 12 months effectively need to raise again before the model is proven.

### Why 18 Months Matters For CLARK

CLARK is trying to prove:

- a training business
- a curriculum business
- a software pilot
- an operating model for partner facilities

That is a lot to prove in 12 months. An 18-month plan is more honest if the objective is to avoid returning to the market too early.

### 18-Month Capital Multipliers

For planning purposes:

- lean 18-month plan: about `1.35x to 1.5x` the lean 12-month plan
- working 18-month plan: about `1.4x to 1.6x` the working 12-month plan
- buffered 18-month plan: about `1.4x to 1.7x` the buffered 12-month plan

### Lean 18-Month Company Case

- `CAD 1.3M to CAD 2.2M`

### Working 18-Month Company Case

- `CAD 2.1M to CAD 3.8M`

### Buffered 18-Month Platform Case

- `CAD 3.4M to CAD 6.0M`

## Monthly Burn Logic

### Months 1 To 3

Expected burn profile:

- high setup spend
- legal, insurance, and structural work
- curriculum and training launch setup
- Clarkware pilot build starts
- little revenue offset

Working monthly burn band:

- `CAD 90,000 to CAD 180,000`

### Months 4 To 6

Expected burn profile:

- staffing and contractor load stabilizes
- training launch begins to offset some spend
- Clarkware pilot work continues
- partner development accelerates

Working monthly burn band:

- `CAD 80,000 to CAD 160,000`

### Months 7 To 12

Expected burn profile:

- training revenue starts to reduce net burn
- Clark Courses contribution should start to matter
- software and node-development work continue consuming capital

Working monthly net burn band:

- `CAD 60,000 to CAD 140,000`

### Months 13 To 18

Expected burn profile:

- depends on whether CLARK keeps hiring
- depends on whether Clarkware is still mostly development spend
- depends on whether node-development work becomes recurring-value work

Working monthly net burn band:

- `CAD 55,000 to CAD 150,000`

Capital should unlock:

- Hamilton operating model executable

### Gate 2: First Revenue And Delivery Rhythm

Capital should unlock:

### Gate 3: Clarkware Pilot Credibility

Capital should unlock:

- live workstation pilot
- evidence capture path
- reporting proof
- realistic support burden estimate

### Gate 4: Partner-Facility Development Credibility

Capital should unlock:

- serious node qualification work
- one or more credible partner pathways

Capital should unlock:

- repeatable training operations
- clearer Clarkware pricing logic
- clearer node-enablement model
- a better basis for the next raise or financing step

## Raise Strategy Interpretation

### If CLARK wants to raise for 12 months only

The most sensible target range is:

- `CAD 1.5M to CAD 2.5M`

This is enough to behave like a real company rather than a thin project, but it still assumes disciplined hiring and no oversized facility contribution.

### If CLARK wants to raise for 18 months with real runway

The most sensible target range is:

- `CAD 2.5M to CAD 4.0M`

This range better matches the ambition to prove multiple interdependent business lines before coming back to market.

### When `CAD 5M` Makes Sense

An early raise around `CAD 5M` becomes defensible if CLARK wants:

- `18 to 24 months` of runway
- `3 founders` on real compensation
- `3 to 5 early staff`
- serious contractor and software support
- meaningful contribution to Clark Ave outfitting
- enough buffer to survive delays without immediate retrenchment

My independent conclusion:

- `CAD 5M` is bold, but not irrational, if the ambition is to build CLARK as a properly capitalized platform company rather than a very lean founder-led experiment

## Recommended Raise Framing

If CLARK wants a raise story that is ambitious but still grounded in the current documentation, the cleanest founder narrative is:

- raising enough to prove the training-first launch
- building Clark Courses into a real education line
- funding Clarkware through real pilot proof
- developing the first partner-facility pathway
- and carrying enough runway to avoid a premature return to market

## Current Recommendation

My best current recommendation is:

- `CAD 2.0M to CAD 3.5M` as the strongest all-around target for a serious 12 to 18 month CLARK plan without overreaching

If the strategy is intentionally bolder, includes deeper staffing, and includes meaningful Clark Ave contribution, then:

- `CAD 4.0M to CAD 5.0M`

is a defensible early target.
