# Investor Selection Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines how CLARK should evaluate a specific investor once that investor is on the radar.

It is not for finding investors.

It is for deciding whether a particular investor belongs in the active target list, the cultivate list, or the reject list.

## Status Labels

- `Priority Target`
- `Cultivate`
- `Watchlist`
- `Do Not Pursue`

## Hard Filters

An investor should usually fail immediately if one or more of these is true:

- does not invest at or near CLARK's current stage
- only invests in pure SaaS or consumer internet
- rejects hardware, manufacturing, industrial, or training-linked business models outright
- requires a geography CLARK is not prepared to commit to
- creates unacceptable strategic conflict with partner facilities or future node relationships
- is known for misaligned governance, predatory terms, or low-reputation behavior

## Core Scoring Dimensions

Score each from `0` to `5`.

### 1. Thesis Fit

- `5`: explicitly invests in industrial software, advanced manufacturing, deep tech, or productivity technology in real-world sectors
- `3`: broad B2B or climate thesis with some plausible overlap
- `0`: little or no real fit

### 2. Stage Fit

- `5`: invests at pre-seed, seed, or proof-stage rounds
- `3`: prefers Series A but occasionally earlier
- `0`: clearly too late-stage

### 3. Check Size Fit

- `5`: can write checks appropriate to CLARK's target round
- `3`: check size may be small or too large but still workable in syndicate
- `0`: structurally mismatched

### 4. Geography Fit

- `5`: explicitly comfortable with Ontario, Canada, Buffalo, or cross-border structures, with added value if they have real reach into `Hamilton`, `Niagara`, `London`, or `Windsor`
- `3`: North America-wide but with no special regional alignment
- `0`: geography is a likely blocker

### 5. Cross-Border Comfort

- `5`: explicit comfort with Canada-U.S. operating complexity
- `3`: no evidence either way
- `0`: strongly domestic-only orientation that conflicts with CLARK's thesis

### 6. Business Model Tolerance

- `5`: can underwrite mixed software, services, training, and industrial coordination
- `3`: accepts some complexity but wants cleaner software positioning
- `0`: expects a single-category business only

### 7. Industrial Credibility

- `5`: team has real industrial, manufacturing, logistics, or deep-tech credibility
- `3`: generic venture background with some relevant portfolio
- `0`: no real industrial understanding

### 8. Public-Capital Compatibility

- `5`: comfortable with grants, matching programs, or public co-investors
- `3`: neutral
- `0`: dislikes public-capital complexity enough to create friction

### 9. Lead Versus Follow Fit

- `5`: role matches what CLARK needs now
- `3`: may fit only as a co-investor
- `0`: role mismatch is severe

### 10. Follow-On Capacity

- `5`: can support later rounds or materially help introduce later capital
- `3`: limited reserves but strong network
- `0`: no meaningful follow-on value

### 11. Speed And Process

- `5`: known for practical early-stage decision speed
- `3`: moderate process burden
- `0`: slow or bureaucratic relative to CLARK's needs

### 12. Value-Add Beyond Money

- `5`: can help with customers, partnerships, talent, strategy, or future fundraising
- `3`: mostly capital only
- `0`: little strategic help

### 13. Reputation And Founder Fit

- `5`: widely regarded as constructive and founder-credible
- `3`: mixed or limited signal
- `0`: problematic reputation

### 14. Conflict Risk

- `5`: low conflict risk
- `3`: manageable conflict risk
- `0`: high likelihood of partner or channel conflict

### 15. Strategic Patience

- `5`: comfortable with proof-building and industrial timelines
- `3`: some patience but pressure for fast category simplification
- `0`: expects instant software-style scaling

## Interpretation

- `60+`: `Priority Target`
- `45 to 59`: `Cultivate`
- `30 to 44`: `Watchlist`
- `Below 30`: `Do Not Pursue`

Scores do not replace judgment. A single hard conflict can still override a high score.

## Profile Template

For each investor, capture:

- firm or person name
- investor type
- stage
- typical check size
- geography
- sectors
- public portfolio signals
- likely lead or follow role
- key partner or contact
- warm intro path
- score
- current status
- next step

## Special Rules For CLARK

### Prefer investors who can understand at least two of these three:

- industrial software
- advanced manufacturing or logistics
- training, workforce, or operational rollout complexity

### Do not overvalue brand name

A famous fund with poor thesis fit is worse than a smaller fund with real industrial understanding.

### Separate strategic value from investor quality

A strategic operator may be a valuable ally but a poor investor.

### Give extra weight to local-regional relevance in Ontario

All else equal, a solid investor with real relationship strength in `Hamilton`, `Niagara`, `London`, or `Windsor` may be more useful to CLARK than a more famous but geographically distant fund.

### Treat public-capital programs differently

Programs like `43North` or `New York Ventures` should be scored for fit, but also for entity and geography requirements, because those can be more important than capital amount.

## Ongoing Review Cadence

- re-score after every meaningful conversation
- re-score when CLARK's financing story changes
- re-score when a program opens, closes, or changes requirements
