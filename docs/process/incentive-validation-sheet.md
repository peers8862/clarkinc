# Incentive Validation Sheet

## Purpose

This sheet narrows CLARK's incentive language to what is currently safe for investor-facing use.

## Safe Current Framing

- Canadian and New York incentive structures create potentially useful support context for CLARK.
- Program applicability depends on entity structure, location, and the actual funded activity.
- Incentives should be presented as upside and support context, not as core economics that make the model work.

## Claim-By-Claim View

| Program Or Claim | Current Read | Investor-Safe Wording | Do Not Say Yet |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SR&ED | Real Canadian program | Eligible Canadian R&D activity may qualify depending on claimant structure and project facts. | SR&ED materially improves CLARK economics today. |
| NRC IRAP | Real Canadian program | IRAP may be relevant if a qualifying Canadian SME is developing commercializable technology. | IRAP is already available to CLARK. |
| FedDev Ontario | Real regional support context | Hamilton sits inside the FedDev Ontario service area and some programs may become relevant. | FedDev support is part of the current committed financial plan. |
| NYPA hydropower | Real program family | Western New York hydropower-linked programs are part of the regional operating context for eligible projects. | Buffalo economics depend on NYPA support. |
| FTZ and Buffalo logistics support | Plausible and partially validated | Buffalo has relevant logistics and site-support context that may help later facility decisions. | CLARK already has project-specific FTZ or logistics benefits locked in. |
| START-UP NY | Closed to new applicants | Do not use. | Any active START-UP NY benefit claim. |

## Current Recommendation

- Keep incentives in the narrative as optional support context.
- Do not treat incentives as a pillar of the raise story.
- Keep the investor story centered on operating proof, training revenue, Clarkware proof, and partner-facility economics.
