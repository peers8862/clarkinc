# Beachhead Customer Decision Memo

## Purpose

This memo settles CLARK's first customer segment for investor-facing planning and business-plan development.

## Decision

CLARK's first commercial segment should be:

- industrial-controls OEMs and integrators needing low-volume, firmware-sensitive PCB and PCBA assembly

This should be treated as the beachhead default across the venture overview, business plan, and pitch.

Adjacent categories such as smart-energy hardware, sensing systems, edge devices, and rugged instrumentation remain relevant expansion lanes, but they should not be presented as equal first-priority segments in investor-facing materials.

## Why This Segment Wins

### 1. Best Fit With CLARK's Current Operating Shape

This segment aligns best with the current CLARK launch model:

- smaller production runs
- high-mix and engineering-adjacent work
- meaningful firmware and test sensitivity
- demand for trusted regional execution rather than lowest-cost volume production

### 2. Best Match To Current Evidence

The repo's current research support is strongest around industrial controls and power-oriented applications, not around a broader and less specific advanced-hardware claim.

### 3. Best Match To The Hamilton Launch Sequence

A training-first launch, followed by Clarkware proof and then partner-facility assembly enablement, is easier to explain against industrial controls than against broad defense or generic EMS categories.

### 4. Best Investor Narrative Discipline

Choosing one first segment removes avoidable ambiguity in:

- who buys first
- what type of jobs CLARK is built to support first
- what kinds of proof points matter in year 1

## Buyer And User

- Buyer: manufacturing managers, operations leaders, procurement leaders, and technical operations leadership inside industrial-controls OEMs and integrators
- User: hardware engineers, embedded teams, production coordinators, test and quality personnel, and plant-facing technical staff

## Typical Program Profile

- low-volume to mid-volume board builds
- high-mix revisions and engineering changes
- firmware loading, test, or evidence-capture sensitivity
- regional or North America-based sourcing preference
- need for faster communication than generic offshore or large-scale EMS paths usually provide

## Why CLARK Is Differentiated For This Segment

- CLARK can pair training, workflow rigor, and partner-facility execution rather than offering only assembly labor.
- Clarkware is more believable when tied to traceability, handoffs, and evidence capture in industrial-controls workflows than when pitched as generic factory software.
- The Hamilton training model supports workforce development that is directly relevant to this customer set.

## What This Decision Does Not Claim

- It does not claim industrial controls is the only eventual segment.
- It does not claim CLARK has already won proof from named customers in this segment.
- It does not claim industrial controls is a fully validated market wedge beyond current evidence strength.
