# Clark Venture Workflow

## Objective

Use this workspace to turn a rough company idea into a coherent business plan and pitch backed by evidence.

## Workflow

1. Start in `docs/venture/venture-overview.md` and define the current thesis.
2. Run the research process in `docs/process/research.md` until the core assumptions have evidence behind them.
3. Promote validated assumptions into `docs/venture/business-plan.md`.
4. Translate the business plan into persuasive narrative in `docs/venture/pitch.md`.
5. Revisit the overview when the target market, problem, or pricing changes.
6. Publish a concise program checkpoint in `docs/program-status-memo.md` after major planning updates.

## Working Rules

- Separate evidence from opinion.
- Mark low-confidence assumptions clearly.
- Prefer bottom-up market logic over top-down vanity numbers.
- Keep the first version narrow enough to be believable.

## Agent Coordination

- `agents/orchestrator.md` sets priorities and resolves conflicts between workstreams.
- `agents/research-agent.md` owns evidence gathering and customer insight synthesis.
- `agents/business-plan-agent.md` owns the operating model and plan coherence.
- `agents/pitch-agent.md` owns the investor-facing and partner-facing narrative.
- `agents/brand-agent.md` owns positioning, messaging tone, and naming logic around Clark.

## Definition Of Done

The first planning cycle is complete when:

- The target customer is clearly named.
- The problem and solution are concrete.
- The market size and competition sections cite real evidence.
- The business model is internally consistent.
- The pitch can explain the venture in under 60 seconds and in a 10 to 12 slide deck.
