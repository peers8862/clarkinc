# Corridor Comparison Memo

## Purpose

This memo gives CLARK an investor-safe corridor position.

## Current Decision

Hamilton-Buffalo remains CLARK's working launch corridor.

The correct claim is:

- Hamilton-Buffalo is credible enough to anchor the first proof case

The incorrect claim is:

- Hamilton-Buffalo is clearly the best possible corridor

## Comparison Standard

The corridor should be judged against realistic alternatives on:

- engineering and manufacturing talent
- logistics practicality
- ability to support a training-first launch
- relevance to the first industrial-controls beachhead
- cross-border or domestic commercial logic

## Corridor Comparison

| Corridor | Strengths | Weaknesses | Recommended Treatment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hamilton-Buffalo | Binational structure, Hamilton engineering depth, Buffalo industrial access, practical cross-border story, strong fit for the current CLARK thesis | More complex operationally than a single-country launch; superiority is not yet proven | Working default |
| Hamilton-Waterloo | Strong Canadian engineering and manufacturing talent concentration; simpler national operating context | Weaker immediate US-dollar demand story and weaker binational differentiation | Strong comparison case, not selected |
| Buffalo-Rochester-Syracuse | Strong US-side advanced-manufacturing and microelectronics narrative; domestic simplicity | Loses the immediate Canada-linked R&D and co-location logic at Hamilton | Strong comparison case, not selected |

## Investor-Safe Position

CLARK should say:

- Hamilton-Buffalo is the current launch corridor because it is credible, coherent with the co-location model, and strong enough to support the first proof sequence

CLARK should not say:

- Hamilton-Buffalo is decisively superior to all alternatives

## Why Hamilton-Buffalo Still Wins The Working Default

- Hamilton already anchors the training-first and Niagara co-location plan.
- Buffalo remains the cleanest current US-side assembly anchor for the repo's operating story.
- The binational structure still matters to CLARK's identity and expansion thesis.

## What Still Needs Validation

- project-specific freight economics
- project-specific customs and handoff burden
- customer-density comparison against alternatives
- the first 24-month expansion map after Hamilton and Buffalo
