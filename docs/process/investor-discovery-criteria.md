# Investor Discovery Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines how CLARK should discover new investor and backer candidates over time.

It is not an evaluation scorecard.

It is a discovery framework for identifying very different kinds of people and institutions that could finance, accelerate, or strategically back CLARK.

## Discovery Principle

CLARK should not search only for “venture capital firms.”

CLARK should search across distinct capital archetypes that match different parts of the business.

Discovery should also stay geographically intentional.

For Canadian investor work, Ontario should receive special emphasis, especially:

- `Hamilton`
- `Niagara`
- `London`
- `Windsor`

## Discovery Archetypes

### 1. Industrial Software Investors

What to look for:

- firms investing in industrial software, industrial AI, digital industrial, IoT, predictive maintenance, factory software, or workflow systems

Why this matters:

- this is the cleanest fit for Clarkware

Signals:

- uses phrases like `industrial software`, `digital industrial`, `manufacturing software`, `AI for operations`, `real-world sectors`
- portfolio includes manufacturing, logistics, energy, field operations, or asset-heavy software

Typical people to identify:

- managing partners
- partners focused on industrials
- platform or portfolio support leads

### 2. Advanced Manufacturing And Deep-Tech Investors

What to look for:

- firms backing advanced manufacturing, robotics, photonics, electronics, devices, materials, or productivity technologies

Why this matters:

- CLARK is not just workflow software; it also sits close to production, tooling, and physical operations

Signals:

- mentions `advanced manufacturing`, `deep tech`, `hardware`, `electronics`, `industrial innovation`
- portfolio includes devices, sensors, robotics, or manufacturing systems

### 3. Public And Quasi-Public Equity Programs

What to look for:

- state, provincial, or public-backed venture programs
- matching funds
- accelerators with equity investment

Why this matters:

- Buffalo-side capital may be easier to access through these channels than through pure private VC at first

Signals:

- matching capital requirement
- relocation or in-state job creation criteria
- equity investment plus ecosystem support

### 4. Strategic Manufacturing Operators

What to look for:

- owners, CEOs, or corridor operators who may invest personally, through a holding company, or through special-purpose strategic structures

Why this matters:

- some of the best CLARK backers may be people who care about corridor strength and workforce depth, not career VCs

Signals:

- multi-node or multi-site manufacturing background
- visible interest in workforce and regional industrial capacity
- prior government-funding, acquisition, or expansion activity

### 5. Family Offices With Industrial, Logistics, Or Real-Asset Bias

What to look for:

- family offices or holding groups tied to manufacturing, industrial services, logistics, or regional development

Why this matters:

- these groups can be more patient with mixed software and real-economy stories than conventional seed funds

Signals:

- principal or family made money in industrial operations, transportation, energy, or infrastructure
- invests in private businesses, growth equity, or special situations

### 6. Workforce And Regional-Economy Backers

What to look for:

- philanthropic, civic, or mission-driven actors that care about workforce development, reindustrialization, and regional entrepreneurship

Why this matters:

- CLARK's training-first wedge makes it legible to ecosystem builders, not just venture investors

Signals:

- funds workforce, entrepreneurship, innovation, advanced manufacturing, or talent pipelines
- participates in regional innovation ecosystems or startup competitions

### 7. Defence And Dual-Use Capital

What to look for:

- investors, programs, or strategics that explicitly back dual-use, secure supply chain, electronics, or manufacturing resilience

Why this matters:

- only relevant if CLARK chooses a real defence or dual-use path

Signals:

- uses phrases like `dual-use`, `defence tech`, `secure supply chain`, `critical industries`

## Discovery Search Terms

Use search combinations such as:

- `industrial software venture capital Canada`
- `advanced manufacturing investor Ontario`
- `Hamilton investor advanced manufacturing`
- `Niagara investor electronics manufacturing`
- `London Ontario angel investor industrial technology`
- `Windsor investor manufacturing technology`
- `Southwestern Ontario family office manufacturing`
- `Buffalo startup investor advanced manufacturing`
- `Upstate New York seed fund manufacturing`
- `family office industrial manufacturing Canada`
- `operator angel electronics manufacturing Ontario`
- `regional innovation fund advanced manufacturing New York`

## Discovery Fields To Capture

For each new candidate, record:

- name
- archetype
- geography
- sectors or thesis
- stage
- typical capital type
- whether they lead or follow
- whether they can support cross-border structures
- likely relevance to training / Clarkware / partner-facility build
- key contact names
- warm intro routes
- why they might care now

## Distinct Sets Of People To Track

CLARK should deliberately profile different human types, not just organizations.

### Set A: Institutional investors

- partners
- principals
- associates who source deals

### Set B: Strategic operators

- owners
- CEOs
- EVPs of business development
- corporate development leaders

### Set C: Ecosystem capital actors

- accelerator investment leads
- public program managers
- venture platform leads
- economic development officers tied to venture or matching programs

### Set D: Operator-angels and regional champions

- exited founders
- manufacturing owners
- logistics operators
- civic entrepreneurs
- family-office principals

## Discovery Workflow

### Weekly

- add new investors from active sectors and corridor geographies
- add at least one new Ontario relationship prospect when possible, with first priority on `Hamilton`, `Niagara`, `London`, and `Windsor`
- review portfolio and thesis pages of target firms

### Monthly

- review investor status against CLARK's current financing story
- prune generic SaaS investors or poor-fit names
- add new public programs, accelerators, or strategic operators

### Quarterly

- refresh all priority contacts
- reassess whether Ontario local-regional relationship building is getting enough attention relative to national-name investors
- reassess whether Buffalo-side or Ontario-side investors should dominate the next quarter

## Red Flags During Discovery

- investor only backs consumer or pure SaaS
- investor clearly avoids hardware or industrial businesses
- investor requires a geography CLARK is not ready to serve
- investor has strong conflict risk with likely node partners
- investor is visible but not actually active at CLARK's stage

## Output Rule

Discovery should always produce one of these:

- `add to selection list`
- `watchlist only`
- `keep for strategic relationships, not capital`
- `discard`
