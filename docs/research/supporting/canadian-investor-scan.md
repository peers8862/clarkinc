# Canadian Investor Scan

## Purpose

This memo supports `docs/financials/investors-and-investment-firms.md` and expands the Canada-only investor research performed on `2026-03-28`.

The goal is not to declare a final target list.

The goal is to identify Canadian investors, angel networks, operator-backed funds, and ecosystem patrons that could plausibly finance CLARK or materially improve access to financing.

Ontario is absolutely in scope for this scan.

Within Ontario, the highest-priority relationship geographies are:

- `Hamilton`
- `Niagara`
- `London`
- `Windsor`

## Framing

CLARK is not yet a clean SaaS story.

The best Canadian capital targets are those that can tolerate some mix of:

- industrial software
- advanced manufacturing
- training and workforce infrastructure
- hardware-adjacent execution
- regional productivity or corridor-building logic

That immediately rules out many generic consumer, fintech, or pure-software seed investors.

## Ontario Relationship Priority

This Canada-only investor work should not drift into an abstract national list.

Ontario remains the most important relationship-building geography for CLARK because it is the current operating base and the most likely first expansion surface.

The scan should therefore place special emphasis on:

- investors based in or active across the Greater Hamilton area
- Niagara-region business builders, angels, and civic-capital actors
- London manufacturing, agtech, medtech, and industrial-tech investors with Southwestern Ontario reach
- Windsor operators, family-business investors, and manufacturing-linked angels tied to automotive, tooling, cross-border industry, or workforce systems

This does not mean only local investors matter.

It means Ontario local and corridor-proximate relationships deserve extra weight because they can provide:

- warmer access
- faster credibility
- better understanding of CLARK's operating context
- more patient support for mixed training, software, and industrial rollout

## Highest-Fit Canadian Candidates Right Now

These are the strongest current Canadian fits from this scan.

### `McRock Capital`

- Why it matters:
  industrial software and industrial AI are at the core of McRock's public thesis, which maps unusually well to Clarkware and later node software.
- Why CLARK is not a perfect fit yet:
  McRock appears strongest once CLARK has clearer software traction and some real operating proof.
- Best use now:
  relationship building and thesis calibration before a formal raise.
- Contact path:
  `deals@mcrockcapital.com`
- Sources:
  https://www.mcrockcapital.com/
  https://www.mcrockcapital.com/reaching-out/

### `BDC Capital Seed Venture Fund`

- Why it matters:
  BDC is still the broadest national institutional route for an early Canadian venture story that blends software and hardware.
- Current public signal:
  BDC describes the Seed Fund as a `CAD 100M` fund investing in software and hardware themes including AI, robotics, IoT / industrials, climate, and defence / dual use.
- Why CLARK should care:
  this is one of the most realistic first institutional conversations for a Canadian entity.
- Main caution:
  CLARK still needs to present as a venture-scale business rather than a training shop.
- Contact path:
  BDC venture intake through fund page.
- Sources:
  https://www.bdc.ca/en/bdc-capital/venture-capital
  https://www.bdc.ca/en/bdc-capital/venture-capital/funds/seed-fund

### `RiSC Capital`

- Why it matters:
  RiSC is one of the cleaner Canada-only deep-tech seed profiles for technical founders working on real-world industrial systems.
- Current public signal:
  RiSC positions itself as "Canada's Deep Tech Venture Fund," says it specializes in Canadian deep-tech startups at pre-seed or seed, and states public funding ranges of `CAD 250K` to `CAD 400K`.
- Why CLARK should care:
  the firm appears more willing than many funds to engage with technical and industrial complexity before full software polish.
- Main caution:
  CLARK's training-first wedge needs to be translated into a more explicit deep-tech and systems story.
- Contact path:
  website contact form.
- Sources:
  https://www.risc.capital/
  https://www.risc.capital/faq
  https://www.risc.capital/our-team

### `i4 Capital`

- Why it matters:

- Current public signal:

- Why CLARK should care:
  one of the better Quebec-based fits if CLARK develops a stronger Industry 4.0 and manufacturing-productivity narrative.
- Main caution:

## What This Scan Changes For CLARK

### Practical conclusion 1

Canada has enough real investor options that CLARK does not need to depend on Buffalo-first capital to start investor development.

### Practical conclusion 2

The best near-term Canadian fit is not one category.

It is a stack:

- one or two industrial or deep-tech funds
- one or two strong angel networks
- a few operator-backed ecosystem patrons

### Practical conclusion 3

Quebec is stronger than expected for this project.

The combination of `Anges Québec`, `AQC Capital`, `i4 Capital`, `Boreal Ventures`, `RiSC Capital`, and Quebec institutional co-investment culture makes it worth treating Quebec as an active investor-discovery geography, not a side note.

### Practical conclusion 4

Vancouver matters less as a direct industrial fit than Quebec, but more than expected as a source of angel and early-stage tech capital through `Version One`, `Vanedge`, `Active Impact`, `Evok`, and `VANTEC`.
