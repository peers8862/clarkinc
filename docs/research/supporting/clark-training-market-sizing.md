IPC Training Market Sizing — Hamilton Facility

Prepared for internal use. March 2025.

This document sizes the reachable IPC training market for Clark's Hamilton facility. It establishes demand assumptions, pricing benchmarks, course economics, three-scenario revenue projections, and the facility fee model inherited from Clark's agreement with Niagara Assembly.

**1. IPC Certification Pricing Benchmarks**

The following pricing data is drawn from EPTAC (the market reference for Ontario), published IPC exam fee schedules effective January 2025–2026, and industry pricing surveys. All CAD figures use a USD/CAD rate of 1.38 (approximate current rate). Clark's own pricing will be positioned at or near EPTAC Markham rates to compete on geography and service, not price.

**1.1 IPC Exam Fees (Pass-Through Cost to Clark)**

These are IPC-set fees paid to the certification body per student — Clark must pass these through as a direct cost regardless of the training format.

|                                      |                                                                          |
|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **IPC-A-610 CIS Exam**               | USD $120 (non-member) / USD $95 (IPC member) per student — online exam   |
| **IPC-A-610 CIS Exam (print)**       | USD $155 (non-member) / USD $130 (IPC member) per student — printed exam |
| **CIT Exam Fees**                    | Approximately 2–3x CIS exam rate, per IPC schedule                       |
| **CAD Equivalent (CIS, non-member)** | \~CAD $166–$214 per student at current exchange                          |
| **Recertification Cycle**            | Every 2 years — mandatory for all CIS, CIT, and CSE holders              |
| **Recertification Cost**             | 60–70% of initial certification fee                                      |

**2. Demand Analysis — Hamilton Corridor**

Two demand pools are identified: corporate/company-sponsored demand (employers paying for their workforce) and individual/institutional demand (students and near-graduation candidates).

**5. Niagara Assembly Facility Fee Model**

The Hamilton training facility is hosted within the Niagara Assembly footprint. The following fee structure is as defined in the Clark business plan. These are direct costs to Clark paid to Niagara Assembly, and are excluded from the direct instructional cost calculation.

|                              |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **1–2 courses/month**        | CAD $1,250 fixed monthly fee + 12% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs                                                                                                                                           |
| **3–4 courses/month**        | CAD $2,500 fixed monthly fee + 10% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs                                                                                                                                           |
| **5+ courses/month**         | CAD $4,000 fixed monthly fee + 8% of contribution margin after direct instructional costs                                                                                                                                            |
| **Scope of facility fee**    | Covers space, equipment access, and site overhead. Does not include instructor liability (Clark bears), trainee injury or equipment damage (Niagara Assembly bears), or facility safety and site procedures (Niagara Assembly owns). |
| **Contribution margin base** | Contribution margin after direct instructional costs only. Broader Clark overhead is excluded from the calculation.                                                                                                                  |

**5.1 Facility Fee Worked Examples**

Base case — 2 courses delivered in a given month, each with 8 students, blended rate CAD $1,200, direct cost rate 55%:

**5.2 Break-Even Analysis**

Clark reaches monthly break-even on direct facility operating costs at the following course volumes (assuming instructor, exam, and materials costs are fully covered by tuition):

|                                             |                                                                                                                                         |
|---------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Minimum students per month (break-even)** | \~12–16 students (1.5–2 classes of 8)                                                                                                   |
| **Minimum courses per month**               | 2 courses of 8 students at CAD $1,100 avg                                                                                               |
| **Monthly fixed costs to cover**            | Instructor fees \~CAD $4,800 + IPC fees \~CAD $2,688 + facility fee \~CAD $1,250 = \~CAD $8,738                                         |
| **Revenue at break-even**                   | 16 students × $1,100 = $17,600 gross → contribution $7,920 → positive after facility fee                                                |
| **Conclusion**                              | Clark reaches monthly operating break-even at approximately 2 classes per month of 8 students each. This is the Year 1 base case floor. |
