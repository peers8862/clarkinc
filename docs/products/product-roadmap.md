# CLARK Product Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines the order in which CLARK's product families should be built, tested, and operationalized.

The near-term priority is not to launch every product line at once. It is to prove the training-first commercial wedge and the Clarkware development and testing path that strengthens the network at the workstation and facility level.

## Roadmap Priorities

1. Training services
2. Clarkware development and testing
3. Assembly services through partner facilities
4. Firmware optimization through partner facilities
5. Logistics and facility coordination services

## Product Family Roles

### Training Services

Training services are the first direct revenue layer.

They include:

- IPC certification delivery
- train-the-trainer services
- Clark Courses wraparound curriculum
- later licensed training-center models

Training services should establish CLARK's first cash flow, workforce credibility, and operating discipline inside the Niagara Assembly relationship.

The training-services family now includes both IPC-centered delivery and Clark Courses as a standalone professional education line with future licensing potential.

### Clarkware

Clarkware is the first core product-development effort.

It should begin as a development and testing program tied to real station activity, evidence capture, messaging, reporting, and auditable human-and-AI coordination. Early success depends on proving the Niagara pilot honestly before broadening the software story.

### Assembly Services

Assembly services should be framed as partner-facility-delivered services, not as CLARK trying to become a centralized contract manufacturer.

Regional specialist facilities carry out the work. CLARK improves their ability to win and deliver work through:

- training and workforce development
- software and workstation tooling
- process standards and documentation
- quality and reporting systems
- customer coordination and network credibility
- selective capital and launch support

### Firmware Optimization

Firmware optimization should also be framed as a network-enabled service carried out through regional specialists and trusted technical partners.

CLARK's role is to create the shared environment, documentation, workflow support, and coordination layer that helps firmware-sensitive manufacturing work happen more effectively inside the network.

### Logistics And Facility Coordination

These services may become highly valuable, but they should follow after the first training and Clarkware proof points.

The likely scope includes:

- cross-border shipment coordination
- warehousing and routing support
- customs and handoff coordination
- facility-to-facility scheduling and escalation support
- shared operating visibility across nodes where justified

## Phased Roadmap

### Phase 1: Training-First Launch

Primary focus:

- launch Hamilton training operations
- generate early IPC and train-the-trainer revenue
- refine Clark Courses and Clark Certified structure
- launch the first Clark Courses public and private cohort model
- document repeatable training operations

Why first:

- fastest path to early revenue
- strongest immediate proof of market demand
- strongest path to workforce credibility and regional trust

### Phase 2: Clarkware Development And Testing

Primary focus:

- stand up the Niagara Clarkware pilot
- prove workstation-level IPE use in real work
- prove evidence capture, messaging, and reporting
- validate auditable human-and-AI participation
- document repeatable pilot and rollout patterns

Why second:

- Clarkware is a major long-term differentiator
- it strengthens both training and partner-facility service delivery
- it should be tested in real operating conditions before being scaled as a platform promise

### Phase 3: Partner-Facility Assembly Service Enablement

Primary focus:

- recruit or formalize partner-facility participation
- document CLARK's assembly enablement model
- support quality, workflow, and reporting improvements
- use Clarkware and training to strengthen partner execution

Why third:

- CLARK should enable regional specialists rather than replace them
- this phase turns the platform story into practical network service delivery

### Phase 4: Partner-Facility Firmware Optimization Enablement

Primary focus:

- define the firmware-support offer inside the network
- support partner specialists with shared tooling and workflow coordination
- tie firmware-sensitive work more tightly to evidence capture and production context

Why fourth:

- strategically important, but dependent on the network and software environment being credible first
- best positioned as a specialist network capability, not an abstract promise

### Phase 5: Logistics And Facility Coordination Expansion

Primary focus:

- build out cross-border routing and warehousing support
- define facility coordination services
- support multi-node scheduling, handoffs, and escalation
- selectively monetize logistics and coordination where the network has real leverage

Why later:

- potentially high value, but easier to sequence after training, software, and partner-service execution are already proven

## Near-Term Build Sequence

### Next 6 Months

- prioritize training-service readiness and Hamilton operating launch
- prioritize Clarkware pilot build, development, and testing
- document the assembly-service and firmware-optimization enablement models

### 6 To 12 Months

- prove repeatable training delivery
- prove Clarkware pilot truthfully in live work
- begin structured onboarding of partner-facility assembly and firmware capabilities

### 12 To 24 Months

- expand Clarkware beyond the first pilot station and node
- scale training services into a more repeatable network offer
- deepen assembly and firmware enablement across partner facilities
- begin selective logistics and facility-coordination services where justified

## Discipline Rule

CLARK should avoid presenting later-phase logistics and coordination services as mature operating products before the training and Clarkware proof points are real.
